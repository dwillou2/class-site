Jeff Tweedy


Muzzle, 2004.



There’s a random
Painted highway
And a muzzle of bees
My sleeves have come unstitched
From climbing your tree

The sun gets passed grin tree to tree
Silently and back to me
A breeze blown through
Pushed up above the sea
Finally next to you

When the dog’s bark get look like they’re smiling
I don’t think they’re mean
Some people get frightened
Despite fences in between
And the green gets passed…

To you as you begin to cry
I said maybe if I leave
You’ll want me to come back home
Or maybe all you mean
Is to sting a silence in me

You’re irresistable when you get mad
Isn’t it sad I’m immune
And I thought it was cute
For you to kiss my purple black eye
Even though I caught it from you

I still think we’re serious
